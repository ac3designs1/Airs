Legislation Reference

Key laws applicable to Next RP — Melbourne Police

Criminal Code

CC s.1Murder — Intentional killing of another person with malice aforethought. Life imprisonment.
CC s.2Manslaughter — Unintentional killing resulting from criminal negligence or recklessness.
CC s.3Assault — Intentional act causing apprehension of immediate harmful or offensive contact.
CC s.4Aggravated Assault — Assault with a deadly weapon or causing serious bodily harm.
CC s.5Assault on Police Officer — Assault upon a law enforcement officer in the performance of duties.
CC s.6Robbery — Taking property from a person using force or threat of force.
CC s.7Kidnapping — Unlawful detention or carrying away of a person against their will.
CC s.8Possession of Illegal Weapon — Unlicensed possession of a restricted or prohibited weapon.

Traffic Act

TA s.1Reckless Driving — Operating a vehicle in a manner dangerous to the public.
TA s.2Driving Under Influence — Operating a vehicle while impaired by alcohol or substances.
TA s.3Failure to Stop — Failing to stop for a police officer when lawfully directed to do so.
TA s.4Unlicensed Driving — Operating a motor vehicle without a valid driver's licence.
TA s.5Unregistered Vehicle — Operating a vehicle that is not registered with the relevant authority.
TA s.6Speeding — Exceeding the posted speed limit on any public road or highway.

Public Security Act

PS s.1Trespassing — Unlawful entry or remaining on private or restricted property.
PS s.2Disorderly Conduct — Behaviour that causes public alarm, nuisance or disturbance.
PS s.3Obstruction of Police — Wilfully obstructing, hindering or resisting a police officer.
PS s.4Breach of Peace — Acting in a way likely to cause a breach of the peace.

Weapons Act

WA s.1Prohibited Weapon — Possession of a prohibited weapon without lawful excuse.
WA s.2Carrying Concealed — Carrying a concealed weapon in a public place without authority.
WA s.3Brandishing — Displaying a weapon in a threatening or intimidating manner.
